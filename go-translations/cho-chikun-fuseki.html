Preface

This is a sequel of sorts to Tsumego at a Glance and Tesuji at a Glance, compiled as a collection of opening problems. However, unlike life-and-death problems, a clear-cut answer - the kind that even professionals deliberate over - is hard to find. Depending on playing style, whether one prefers thickness or prioritizes territory for example, the answer may vary. In that sense, there is also meaning in being able to sketch one’s own picture of the board, and one could say there is just as much to enjoy as there is to puzzle over.

That said, at the opening stage, distinguishing between "big points" and "urgent points" is essential regardless of playing style. This book includes professional games, so some problems may leave you uncertain. But if you find yourself thinking, "So this kind of move is also possible!" - that is enough. By working through these problems repeatedly, your strength to break through to shodan will develop. I would be grateful if this proves of even some help to everyone in cultivating their sense of the opening.

Cho Chikun
