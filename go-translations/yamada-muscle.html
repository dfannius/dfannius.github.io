Preface

When you engage in physical activity such as sports, warm-up exercises are essential. If you neglect your preparation, you are more likely to injure yourself or to get poor results.

Go is the same. Professionals practice reading every day with tsumego and other exercises so as not to dull their instincts. Slacking off even a little dulls your intuition and affects your games.

Go, too, requires training.

For this book, I have carefully selected problems from two of my books published in 2010, "How to Break Down and Fix the Shape of Stones" and "Yamada-Style Training to Multiply Your Go Strength Tenfold" — problems on tesuji, the shape of stones, how to capture stones, how to defend, and other topics that form the foundation of Go strength and will be especially useful for your game. This one volume should be all you need to build your basic Go fitness.

Many of the problems have long sequences before you reach the correct answer. If you cannot work one out, feel free to look at the answer right away. After seeing the correct answer, return to the problem

4

diagram, and if you picture the sequence in your head, you will gradually get the knack of it.

In addition to problems that train your reading, the book also poses problems from a variety of angles — how to find weaknesses in a shape, how to make use of them in actual games, and so on — so I think you will enjoy solving them.

I recommend keeping this book close at hand and working through it a little at a time whenever you have a spare moment.

By practicing the problems in this book repeatedly, I am sure your playing strength will improve dramatically. As you become stronger, the way you see the game will change. When a new world comes into view, Go will appear even more fascinating.

I hope that this book lets you experience the joy of becoming stronger at Go.

August 2014

Yamada Kimio, 9-dan

Chapter 1
Reading Ladders
8

Situations that call for reading out a ladder appear frequently in actual games. Ladders progress in a regular pattern, so they are ideal training for reading. Read them out while watching for ladder breakers. If you cannot read one to the end, it is fine to look at the answer right away. If you try the problem again while picturing the correct answer in your head, you will gradually become able to read them out.

The first key point is to give atari with Black 1 and 3, then cut with 5.

Read out the path to casting a good net and decisively finishing off the stones. As you work through a number of these problems, you should get the knack of it.
