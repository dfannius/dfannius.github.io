The Exemplary Person Is Not a Vessel

The Analects says: “The exemplary person is not a vessel.” This resonates deeply with me. I would like to share some thoughts on this.

An exemplary person can be understood as someone who is well-rounded. A vessel can hold things, but only what it is suited for. We work hard to study Go, and as a result we become strong players, but this only means we are stronger than others in the realm of Go. Everyone has their own specialty, their own area of expertise. I believe the saying “the exemplary person is not a vessel” has a deeper meaning.

Those with strong Go skills receive respect from people, but this should not be enough to satisfy us Go players. We must go further, to reach the level of “not being a vessel.” Beyond the study of Go technique, we should also cultivate ourselves equally in how we treat others and conduct ourselves in the world — we cannot confine ourselves to a narrow technical domain.

Is this not a very important matter for the cultivation of the way of Go?
