12. Eye Space in Capturing Races
Example 1

A 1-point eye space counts as 1 liberty.

A 2-point eye space counts as 2 liberties.

A 3-point eye space counts as 3 liberties.

Example 2

A 2-point eye space with 1 White stone inside counts as 1 liberty.

A 3-point eye space with 1 White stone inside counts as 2 liberties.

A 3-point eye space with 2 White stones inside counts as 1 liberty.

Let’s Learn — How to Improve at Go

  1. Always do a post-game review after each game. By reviewing, you can reflect on your moves and receive guidance from stronger players on where you went wrong and where you played well. You can also go through your own game record quietly to identify and address your weaknesses. No one has ever improved their Go strength without doing post-game reviews.

  2. Replay games by professional players. Professional game records can easily be found in newspapers and Go magazines. As you replay the professionals’ moves one by one, thinking about the meaning of each move, you will find your strength improving significantly before you know it. As you continue replaying game records, you will eventually be able to memorize an entire game — there are players who say they reached 1-kyu by memorizing 10 professional game records. If replaying an entire game is too difficult, studying model fuseki is a great help in improving your strength.
