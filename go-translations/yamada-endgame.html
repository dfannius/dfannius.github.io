Introduction

Everyone who loves Go wants to get stronger - and I am no exception. Go has three stages: the opening (fuseki), the middle game (fighting), and the endgame (yose). The path to improvement differs for each of them.

The area where you can improve most quickly is yose. Yose has patterns, and with practice you will reliably grasp the techniques and be able to apply them in actual games right away.

In my games against amateurs, it is not uncommon to see losses of 20 or 30 points in yose. If you lose 3 points in each of 10 places, that is a 30-point swing right there. In terms of handicap stones, that is a 2 or 3 stone difference, so mastering yose technique directly translates to rank advancement.

The most important thing in yose is taking sente. Even if you give up a little territory, by consistently maintaining sente you can rack up gain after gain in the endgame. On the other hand,

3

preventing the opponent’s sente yose is also important. The purpose of this book is to provide training across all aspects of yose. With that in mind, I have created a wide range of problems, focusing on techniques for taking sente and methods for preventing the opponent’s sente, as well as small tricks for gaining points and immediately applicable moves. The problems range from those aimed at kyu players to those that even dan players will enjoy, covering a wide range of difficulty levels. I have also created similar problems at varying difficulty, so if you work through them in order, I think you will begin to have "Ah, is that the tesuji here?" moments. If you work through the problems in this book repeatedly and master the techniques, you will surely gain 10 points from yose alone.

I hope this book will be of help in your improvement.

Spring 2013

9-dan Yamada Kimio

Problem 1
Black to play

Protect the two marked Black stones. Which move gains the most territory?

8
Correct Answer

The jump of Black 1 is the best move. After White 2 and Black 3, things settle. Black then has the right to crawl at a.

Correct Answer Variation

If White blocks at 2, Black connects at 3, and can make 2 points here.

Failure

Defending with Black 1 allows White to give atari at 2, losing 2 points. Black 1 at a is effective when you want to make eye shape, but after White 2 and Black 3, it is inferior to the correct answer.

Problem 2
Black to play

White has descended to the first line, which is a concern. Without incurring any loss, capture the marked stone 1 in the interior.

10
Correct Answer

The atari of Black 1 makes good shape. Black can block at 3 in response to White 2. If White plays at a instead, Black’s capture at b is fine.

Failure 1

If Black jumps at 1, White wedges in from 2 through 4, and through 6 the Black stones end up captured instead.

Failure 2

If Black tries to capture with the net at 1, the attachment of White 2 is a good move. White connects under through 4, and Black suffers a large loss.

Problem 3
Black to play

There is a capturing race, but since it is Black’s turn, the three marked stones can be captured. Find the most profitable way to capture them.

12
Correct Answer

The kosumi of Black 1 is the profitable way to play in terms of territory. In response to the descent of White 2, Black can connect at 3.

Failure 1

If Black descends at 1, White makes a diagonal attachment at 2, and the four Black stones end up captured instead.

Failure 2

If Black gives atari at 1, White gives atari back at 2. This is 2 points worse than the correct answer.

Problem 4
Black to play

Capture the two marked stones with one more move. Not just any move will do — there is a more profitable way to capture.

14
Correct Answer

The hanging connection of Black 1 is the profitable move. After White 2, Black 3 wins the capturing race.

Failure 1

If Black plays at 1, White cuts at 2 and captures 1 stone.

Failure 2

If Black connects solidly at 1, the two White stones cannot be captured. On the contrary, after White closes in at 2 and 4, through the stone tower squeeze at 10, Black ends up captured instead.
