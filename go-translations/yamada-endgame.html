Introduction

Everyone who loves Go wants to get stronger - and I am no exception. Go has three stages: the opening (fuseki), the middle game (fighting), and the endgame (yose). The path to improvement differs for each of them.

The area where you can improve most quickly is yose. Yose has patterns, and with practice you will reliably grasp the techniques and be able to apply them in actual games right away.

In my games against amateurs, it is not uncommon to see losses of 20 or 30 points in yose. If you lose 3 points in each of 10 places, that is a 30-point swing right there. In terms of handicap stones, that is a 2 or 3 stone difference, so mastering yose technique directly translates to rank advancement.

The most important thing in yose is taking sente. Even if you give up a little territory, by consistently maintaining sente you can rack up gain after gain in the endgame. On the other hand,

3

preventing the opponent’s sente yose is also important. The purpose of this book is to provide training across all aspects of yose. With that in mind, I have created a wide range of problems, focusing on techniques for taking sente and methods for preventing the opponent’s sente, as well as small tricks for gaining points and immediately applicable moves. The problems range from those aimed at kyu players to those that even dan players will enjoy, covering a wide range of difficulty levels. I have also created similar problems at varying difficulty, so if you work through them in order, I think you will begin to have "Ah, is that the tesuji here?" moments. If you work through the problems in this book repeatedly and master the techniques, you will surely gain 10 points from yose alone.

I hope this book will be of help in your improvement.
