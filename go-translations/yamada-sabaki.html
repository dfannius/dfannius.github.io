Introduction

The area where differences in Go strength show up most dramatically is, without question, the middle game. A single attacking decision can swing the position dramatically, and one wrong defensive move can end the game in an instant.

The area that amateur players find particularly difficult is surely "stone handling" — sabaki. When your stones are attacked, the instinct is to run desperately for fear of losing them, and before you know it the opponent’s territory has been steadily growing. You have surely had this experience.

This book develops your skills through a large number of problems, covering everything from the basics to applied sabaki techniques. Start by mastering the fundamentals of sabaki in Chapter 1. The fundamentals of sabaki are:

  1. Make use of sacrifice stones
3
  1. Don’t give atari mindlessly
  2. Use stones diagonally to secure eye shape
  3. Aim for early settlement

These are the four principles. Rather than trying to run with every stone, keep in mind the importance of identifying the key stones and moving out quickly and lightly.

Chapters 2 and 3 cover applied techniques. Chapter 4 presents problems in actual game format. If you have internalized this book, you will find yourself able to see the sabaki tesuji without hints.

Practice the problems in this book repeatedly, and when the sabaki tesuji starts to spring to mind instantly, your fighting strength in actual games will improve dramatically. I will have no greater joy than if this book helps your improvement.
