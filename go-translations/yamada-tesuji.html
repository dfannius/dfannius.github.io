Preface

Studying tesuji is essential for improving at Go, but simply working through problem collections builds only fragmentary knowledge and is not efficient. I myself have worked on many problem collections, and while thinking about whether there was a more efficient way to learn, I noticed something.

The fact is, the tesuji that are used frequently are largely fixed. Not only professionals, but also AI — which is rapidly becoming stronger — selects appropriate moves from the representative tesuji it has mastered and uses them in combination in actual games.

Take the cut or the attach, for example. If you master the patterns in which they are used, the right move will flash to mind instantly — not only when capturing stones, but in every situation: sabaki, connection, and more.

In this book, you will study the representative tesuji intensively, organized by pattern. Some problems may feel difficult, but every one of them appears in actual games, so please review them repeatedly until they are memorized.

After that, when you tackle the actual-game problems in the final chapter, the answers should come to you instantly.

Nothing would make me happier than if this book contributes even a little to your improvement at Go.

March 2017

Yamada Kimio, 9-dan
