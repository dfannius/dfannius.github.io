Other Names for Go

Go has many other names: zuòyǐn (sitting in seclusion), shǒután (conversing with the hands), fāngyuán (square and round), làn kē (the rotted axe handle), niǎolù zhī zhēng (the contention of crows and egrets), and others.

The Shishuo Xinyu says: "Wang Zhonglang called Go zuòyǐn; Zhi Daolin called it shǒután." Both zuòyǐn and shǒután mean playing Go.

During the Meiji era, Murase Shuho founded the Hoensha (方圓社); the book Zuoyin Congtan records this fact in Japanese Go history.

The "fāng" (square) in fāngyuán represents the four corners of the board, while the stones are round in shape — hence the name "square and round."

Shǒután is a particularly evocative term. Its meaning is that through Go one may find a lifelong kindred spirit.
