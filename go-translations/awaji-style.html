Preface

When I watch games played by amateur players, I often see cases where they are losing out simply because they don’t know the correct shapes. Learning the right moves and avoiding bad ones are two sides of the same coin.

However, it is surprisingly difficult to recognize on your own what is a good move and what is a bad one. A mistake like missing an atari is easy to catch because your stones get captured right away, but the damage from playing bad shape doesn’t immediately show up in the results, so it’s hard to notice.

In this book, I have taken actual amateur games as my material and tried to introduce the bad moves that many players are prone to make, explaining how they lead to trouble. By playing even slightly better moves, you can reduce how often your position deteriorates, and that will translate into improvement. I would be glad if this book proves helpful in raising your level of play.

January 2017

Awaji Shuzo
