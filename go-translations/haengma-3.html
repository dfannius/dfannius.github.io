Preface

Children who are just beginning to learn Go, as well as those who have learned the game to some degree, often play haphazardly in actual games without systematically learning the haengma and vital points commonly used in practice. This is a very bad habit for playing Go, and one that does not easily change even later on.

In Go, after the opening — where fuseki and joseki have been systematized — the most essential elements for entering full-scale middlegame fighting and playing efficiently are haengma and vital points.

There was no problem collection systematically compiling haengma and vital points for children, and it is out of this necessity that the present Haengma and Vital Points series was created.

3

In addition, Haengma and Vital Points volumes 1 through 4 are divided into problems for beginners, intermediate players, advanced players, and dan-level players respectively, so children at various levels can study at their own level. Since similar problems are repeated for review, readers will be able to apply appropriate haengma and vital points across a wide range of situations.

The most important thing in actual games is efficient haengma fighting, so by repeatedly studying the various types of haengma through this book and internalizing the fundamentals, I am confident readers will be able to play Go correctly. I would also like to express my gratitude to President Yang of Hyeonwongak Publishing and all the staff for their hard work in bringing this book to publication.

April 1999 Professional 9-dan Seo Neung-wuk
