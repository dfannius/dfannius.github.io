Preface

When we play a game of Go, we cannot play by building territory alone. As both sides go about building territory, one side will inevitably end up with less than the other. The player with less territory will naturally abandon territory-building and instead try to capture the opponent’s groups or attack their stones. Fighting is therefore inevitable.

And in such fighting, capturing races are bound to occur. Whether you can win a fight is thus essentially the same as whether you can win a capturing race.

A capturing race may take the simple form of filling liberties one by one to see who captures first, but depending on the situation it often requires subtle move sequences and high-level reading. Change just one move and a stone that should have been captured might live instead. When a capturing race breaks out between key stones in particular, if your reading is weak and you lose the local fight, the damage can be fatal and cost you the game.

This book is for dan-level players; while some capturing races here are straightforward, many are quite difficult, and you may need to invest considerable time and effort. Since failing to capture means being captured yourself, you must read persistently. As you do, your strength will improve beyond recognition.

Through this fourth volume on capturing races, master the key points and vital spots related to capturing races, gain confidence in your ability to handle them, and crown your games with victory.

— Pro 9-dan Si Neung-ok
