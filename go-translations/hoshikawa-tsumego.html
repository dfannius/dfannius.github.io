Preface

My name is Hoshikawa Kōyō, of the Kansai Ki-in. I began composing tsumego while I was a middle school student in the insei program. This was entirely due to a fellow insei of mine at the Kansai Ki-in, Hatanaka Hoshinobu. His tsumego are simply brilliant — there is no other word for it. Having such a wonderful tsumego composer close at hand, I was quickly drawn into the world of tsumego.

Tsumego are a highly recommended study method. They are the finest textbook available for training your reading ability while also learning tesuji and vital points.

When teaching amateurs, I can impart ideas and strategy, but I cannot strengthen their reading for them. Tsumego is basic training, and it is something only you yourself can do. With the foundation built through tsumego practice, you will absorb other study methods more effectively — which is why I recommend it so strongly.

The first half of this book covers tsumego tesuji and their applications; the second half moves into advanced applications and more difficult tesuji, with the difficulty increasing step by step. For problems you cannot solve after a little thought, please feel free to look at the answer right away. Rather than struggling through a problem, seeing the answer and thinking "Aha, I see!" will do more for your improvement.

This book was conceived around the theme of "practical positions that feel familiar yet have never been seen before." Many of the problems here have drawn reactions from fellow professionals such as "I’ve never seen this tesuji before." Nothing would give me greater joy than conveying even a little of the appeal of tsumego to my readers.

Finally, I would like to express my deep gratitude to Mynavi and to my editor for their tremendous support in making this publication possible.

Hoshikawa Kōyō
